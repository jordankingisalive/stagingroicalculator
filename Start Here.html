Microsoft 365 Copilot Adoption Journey

Based on Microsoft's official Copilot adoption framework

NOVICE USERS
Early Adoption Phase
Actions/User/Week <15
Consistency <9 of 12 weeks
Target Build habit

Characteristics: Initial rollout, awareness building, trial usage. Users exploring features but haven't yet used Copilot consistently (less than 3 out of 4 weeks).

Source: Microsoft Copilot Adoption Report

HABITUAL USERS
Consistent Usage Phase
Actions/User/Week 1-14
Consistency ≥9 of 12 weeks
Frequency 3+ weeks/month

Characteristics: Habit formed. Users integrate Copilot into regular workflows and return consistently—using it at least 3 out of every 4 weeks.

Source: Microsoft Copilot Adoption Report

POWER USERS
Advanced Adoption Phase
Actions/User/Week ≥15
Consistency ≥9 of 12 weeks
Frequency 3+ weeks/month

Characteristics: Deep integration with frequent, consistent use. Advanced features adopted. Driving high-value AI scenarios and workflow transformation.

Source: Microsoft Copilot Adoption Report

📊 About These Definitions

These user classifications come directly from Microsoft's official Copilot Adoption Report methodology, used by thousands of organizations to measure Copilot adoption. The thresholds (15+ actions/week, 75%+ active weeks) are based on Microsoft's research showing that users who reach these levels have a high likelihood of becoming consistent long-term Copilot users.

🎯 Power User Threshold

Microsoft defines Power Users as those taking 15+ Copilot actions per week and using Copilot consistently—meaning they're active at least 9 out of the past 12 weeks. These users demonstrate deep integration and high-value AI scenario adoption across Microsoft 365 apps.

Source: Copilot Usage Level Overview

💡 Measuring What Matters

Microsoft recommends tracking: percentage of active Copilot users, actions per user per week, percentage of power users, and feature adoption by app. Use the Copilot Dashboard in Viva Insights to compare your organization to industry benchmarks.

Source: Copilot Dashboard

⚠️ Watch for Stalled Adoption

Focus interventions on Novice users—those who aren't yet using Copilot consistently (less than 9 out of 12 weeks active). Microsoft's framework identifies these users as needing guided learning experiences, training sessions, and manager-led examples to build confidence and regular usage habits.

Source: Novice Groups Guidance

📈 Learn from Power Groups

Microsoft recommends identifying Power Groups (15+ actions/week, 75%+ active weeks) and showcasing their wins. Share their proven Copilot workflows through demos, repeatable prompts, and playbooks to scale success across the organization.
